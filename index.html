Learn the Basics

Simple explanations for the terms behind your results
🎨

Personal Color

Your personal color is the family of shades that naturally complement your skin, hair and eyes. Wearing colors from your palette tends to make your complexion look brighter and more even — wearing colors outside it can wash you out or add shadows.

🌤️

Undertone

Undertone is the subtle color beneath your skin's surface — warm (yellow/golden), cool (pink/blue), or neutral (a mix of both). It doesn't change with a tan or season, and it's the biggest factor in whether gold or silver jewelry, and warm or cool makeup, suits you best.

🌗

Depth

Depth describes how light or dark your overall coloring is — your skin, hair and eyes together. Lighter depths usually suit softer, lighter shades, while deeper depths can carry richer, more saturated colors without being overwhelmed.

✨

Chroma

Chroma is how bright or muted a color appears. Some people look best in clear, vivid colors (high chroma), while others suit softer, dustier tones (low chroma) — wearing the wrong chroma can make a color look slightly "off" even if the hue itself is right.

⚖️

Contrast

Contrast is the difference in lightness between your hair, skin and eyes. High contrast (e.g. dark hair, light skin) suits bold, defined makeup and color combinations, while low contrast often looks best with softer, more blended tones.

🪞

Why It Matters For Makeup

Foundation should match your depth and undertone so it disappears into your skin. Blush, lips and eyeshadow look most natural when their chroma matches yours — bright if you're bright, soft if you're soft — so your whole face reads as one harmonious palette.
